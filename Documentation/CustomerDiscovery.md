# Customer Discovery Weekly Report

## Project Title
AdEthix AI – AI-Driven Ethical Filter for Marketing Content

---

## Problem Statement
With the increasing use of AI tools in marketing and advertising content creation, there is a growing risk of unintentional bias, stereotypes, or culturally insensitive messaging being generated and published. Many companies rely on AI tools to generate captions, advertisements, and campaign ideas, but there is currently no dedicated system that automatically detects ethical concerns in marketing content before publication.

AdEthix AI aims to develop an AI-powered ethical filtering system that analyzes marketing content and identifies potential ethical risks such as bias, stereotypes, cultural insensitivity, and misleading messaging before the content is published.

---

## Objective
The main objectives of AdEthix AI are:

- Detect bias and stereotypes in AI-generated marketing content  
- Ensure ethical and culturally sensitive communication in advertising  
- Assist marketing teams in reviewing AI-generated content before publishing  
- Reduce reputational risk caused by unethical or insensitive advertisements  

---

## Activities Completed This Week
During this week, the focus was on customer discovery and problem validation to understand whether the proposed solution solves a real-world problem.

Key activities included:

- Identifying professionals working in advertising, marketing, and PR industries

  - How AI tools are used in marketing  
  - Whether bias appears in marketing campaigns

---

## Customer Discovery Insights

### 1. AI Usage in Marketing
Industry professionals confirmed that many marketing teams already use AI tools such as ChatGPT for:

- Caption generation  
- Advertising copywriting  
- Campaign brainstorming  

However, AI tools are typically used as support tools rather than replacing human creativity.

---

### 2. Risk of Bias and Stereotypes
Marketing professionals highlighted that bias and stereotypes can appear unintentionally in marketing content, especially when teams do not review campaigns from multiple perspectives.

This indicates the need for systems that help identify ethical issues early in the content creation process.

---

### 3. Consumer Awareness
Consumers today are highly aware of social and cultural issues, especially on social media platforms.

If marketing content appears culturally insensitive or stereotypical, audiences are quick to call it out. This can damage brand reputation.

Therefore, companies must ensure ethical marketing communication.

---

### 4. Existing Review Process
In most organizations, marketing content goes through multiple levels of review, such as:

- Creative team review  
- Brand or marketing manager approval  
- Legal or compliance review (for larger companies)  

However, these reviews are manual and time-consuming, which creates an opportunity for AI-powered ethical filtering tools like AdEthix AI.
