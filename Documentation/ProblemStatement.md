
# Problem Definition Document

## 1. Problem Statement
AI-generated marketing content often contains unintended gender, cultural, or socio-economic bias, which can harm brand reputation and audience trust. There is a lack of automated tools that can detect and correct such biases before content is published.

---

## 2. Problem Description
With the increasing use of AI tools for generating marketing content such as advertisements, social media captions, and product descriptions, there is a growing risk of unintentionally producing biased or culturally insensitive content.

### Problems Faced by Customers
- Gender-stereotyped phrases  
- Culturally insensitive wording  
- Socio-economic or appearance-based bias  

### Impact
- Negative audience reactions  
- Social media backlash  
- Damage to brand reputation  

---

## Typical Customers
- Digital Marketing Managers  
- Content Writers / Strategists  
- Social Media Managers  
- Brand Managers  
- Influencers  

---

## Why This is a Problem
- AI tools are widely used  
- Bias can appear unintentionally  
- Manual review may miss subtle issues  
- Audience sensitivity is increasing  

---

## Validation from Customer Interactions
- Professionals confirmed usage of AI tools  
- Acknowledged bias issues  
- Manual review is not enough  
- Demand for automated solution exists  

> “Sometimes content may appear fine internally, but audiences react negatively if messaging is unintentionally insensitive.”

---

## Will Customers Pay?
- Helps improve brand safety  
- Prevents reputational damage  
- Useful for large campaigns and global audiences
